Square Peg, Round Hole



‘Square Peg, Round Hole’ is a short film that Inigo Townsend and I did the costume design and supervision for in October 2020.

The story follows a 12-year-old girl named M and her single mother Abbie who is struggling to get her talented and creative daughter into secondary school due to her learning difficulties and stress disorder. In the end of the film, all the frustration and anger the mother and daughter had toward each other is relieved when M makes artwork out of a mess the two created in a paint fight with each other. This leads to Abbie understanding M for who she is and gets her into a school that accepts M for her creative self. I enjoyed this project because when I was growing up with my own learning difficulties, I felt underrepresented alone, finally there is a film for children like me to feel like they belong somewhere for their creativity.



This was made during the COVID-19 pandemic, so I learned about handling my job within restrictions. Navigating how to work with a child on set and keep continuity was the most challenging part of the project during the filming days.
